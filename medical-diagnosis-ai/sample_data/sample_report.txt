CLINICAL FINDINGS:

- Fever pattern consistent with bacterial infection

ASSESSMENT:
Based on clinical presentation and physical examination findings, patient likely has community-acquired pneumonia affecting the right lower lobe. Differential diagnosis includes bacterial pneumonia, viral pneumonia, or atypical pneumonia.

PLAN:
1. Chest X-ray to confirm diagnosis
2. Complete blood count and blood cultures
3. Sputum culture and sensitivity
4. Start empirical antibiotic therapy
5. Supportive care with rest and hydration
6. Follow-up in 48-72 hours

RECOMMENDATIONS:
